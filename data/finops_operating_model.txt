FinOps Operating Model

The FinOps operating model defines how engineering, finance, and product teams collaborate to manage cloud cost efficiency. It provides a framework of roles, responsibilities, processes, and cultural principles.

Key Principles

FinOps is built on principles such as:

Teams must collaborate

Decisions are driven by business value

Every team takes ownership of cloud usage

Cost transparency is essential

FinOps is a continuous lifecycle

Three Phases of FinOps

Inform
Establish visibility, allocate costs, and generate dashboards to understand consumption and spending patterns.

Optimize
Identify idle resources, rightsizing opportunities, discount commitments, reserved capacity planning, and architectural improvements.

Operate
Implement governance processes, track KPIs, and ensure ongoing accountability through operational reviews.

Cost Optimization Techniques

FinOps teams adopt techniques such as:

Rightsizing and autoscaling

Commitment-based discounts

Spot instances

Storage lifecycle policies

Architecture modernization

FinOps KPIs

Common KPIs include:

Cost per customer

Cost per workload

Cloud unit economics

Reserved instance coverage

Savings plan utilization

Cloud efficiency score

The operating model is successful only when teams work together and align cost decisions with business outcomes.
